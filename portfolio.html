2020

Z Hacks 2020 Winner

* The third hackathon I ever attended, Z-Hacks 2020, had 360 participants. This hackathon was different because it is the first hackathon in the world to ever accept non-coding projects.
* We wanted to create a platform that had a massive impact on the world. We decided on the new project, Storiology. Storiology is actually an older idea that we had since we were 7, 7, and 9 years old. It is based on the observation that when kids are hospitalized, instead of offering the kids interesting books to occupy them, hospitals generally give them toys and video games. We wanted ill kids to build good reading habits opposed to being hooked on toys, which is the focus of Storiology; kids reading for sick kids in the hospital. We designed a platform that had to different account options, user and volunteer. Users could choose which book they wanted to be read to them and a volunteer can do it. The software would help them get into touch and they can have a fun time reading together.


https://www.santacruzworks.org/z-hacks-2020
